Employee Benefits Review — Recommendations and Observations

Further to our recent discussions, I have set out below my recommendations and observations regarding the current employee benefits structure, together with the areas where I believe the overall proposition can be strengthened for both the business and employees.

The objective of the review is not simply compliance. The focus is improving employee value, increasing efficiency, strengthening retention, and ensuring the benefits structure remains commercially effective as the business grows.
1 Workplace Pension
Current arrangement — People's Pension

People's Pension is a well-known and widely used auto-enrolment provider that works effectively for straightforward workplace pension administration. The scheme is simple to manage and provides a compliant entry-level pension solution for employers.

However, there are several areas where I believe the overall proposition can be improved.

Key observations

The current charging structure includes:

  • 0.50% Annual Management Charge
  • £6.50 annual member fee

While this remains competitive for smaller pension balances, it becomes less efficient as employee pension values increase. The scheme also offers a relatively limited investment range compared to other providers, which can restrict longer-term flexibility and employee engagement.

That said, the default global investment strategies have performed reasonably well and are managed by respected fund managers including Invesco, Amundi, and State Street.

The issue is not that the scheme is poor, but that there are stronger and more commercially effective alternatives available.

Recommendation
Recommended action

I recommend reviewing the scheme against providers such as Royal London, where I would expect improvements in overall member value, investment flexibility, employee engagement tools, retirement planning functionality, and long-term governance support. Royal London's ProfitShare model may also provide additional long-term value to employees.

Salary Exchange

I would strongly recommend implementing Salary Exchange as part of any revised pension structure.

Based on the current payroll profile:

  • Average salary is approximately £22,400
  • Average monthly contribution is approximately £180

The current structure appears highly suited to Salary Exchange. This would:

  • Reduce Employee National Insurance contributions
  • Create Employer NI savings estimated at approximately £5,400 per annum
  • Improve pension efficiency without materially increasing employer cost

The Employer NI savings can then be redirected into enhanced employer contributions, wider employee benefits, or retained business savings.

Importantly, most employees should remain comfortably above minimum thresholds, even allowing for future legislative changes anticipated from April 2029. It is also increasingly likely that minimum pension contribution requirements will rise over time. Implementing a more efficient structure now places the business in a stronger long-term position.

2 Group Life Insurance
Recommendation — Group Life Cover

I recommend implementing a Group Life Insurance arrangement providing a Death in Service benefit of 4x salary minimum.

This remains one of the most valued and cost-effective employee protection benefits available and materially strengthens the overall employee benefits proposition. Key advantages include:

  • Meaningful family protection
  • Improved recruitment positioning
  • Stronger employee retention
  • Relatively low employer cost compared to perceived employee value
Critical Illness Cover

We will also obtain pricing for Group Critical Illness Cover. However, my expectation is that the cost is likely to prove disproportionately high relative to the value delivered, particularly given the workforce profile and taxable P11D implications for employees.

Alternative — Group Income Protection

A scaled Group Income Protection arrangement is likely to provide better long-term value and practicality than full Critical Illness cover. This would:

  • Provide meaningful support during long-term illness absence
  • Strengthen the wider wellbeing proposition
  • Support absence management and workforce stability

We will obtain quotations for both options for comparison.

3 Private Medical Insurance
Recommendation — Senior Staff Medical Cover

Private Medical Insurance for approximately 12 senior members of staff is a sensible addition to the wider benefits structure.

For leadership and key operational staff, PMI typically delivers strong practical and perceived value through:

  • Faster access to diagnosis and treatment
  • Reduced operational disruption
  • Improved retention
  • Stronger employee confidence

I will source and compare suitable provider options based on level of cover required, excess structure, outpatient limits, employee demographics, and overall budget parameters.

4 Wider Employee Incentives and Benefits

Alongside the core pension and insured benefits, I also recommend continuing to strengthen the wider employee engagement and wellbeing proposition.

As discussed, I have already introduced you to Shop St as part of this process. I believe this is an excellent addition to the overall benefits structure — straightforward to implement, low maintenance from an employer perspective, and delivering immediate day-to-day value to employees through discounts and savings.

I will also introduce a 24/7 GP and wellbeing support service powered by 360 Wellbeing, which I believe will complement the wider employee wellbeing strategy particularly well. This type of support can materially improve:

  • Employee engagement
  • Wellbeing support accessibility
  • Perceived employer value
  • Retention and workforce stability

Importantly, these types of benefits often deliver strong employee value without creating significant additional employer cost or administration.
